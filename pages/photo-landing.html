Photographic Projects

Making photographs is a big part of who I am. I love being able to create something that no one has ever seen and share the way I see the world.

The reason I take photos

I love photography. I enjoy the process of taking a photograph and sharing the final product with my friends and family. I love being able to see the world through the lens and show others the things that I find fascinating. Here are some projects that I have shot as an amateur photographer.

[NO]Reality

This project shows reality extending to a point where we doubt its veracity. I attempt to capture movement, shadow, distortion, and most importantly symmetric compositions, where three-dimensional objects are flattened into two-dimensional geometrical compositions. The idea is to "break" reality by choosing what, how, and when to photograph.

Portraits

This project focuses on students from Brandeis University. The idea was to show what it means for us, as students, to be part of the university and college life. I attempt to show people as I know them, revealing something about them that you woudln't necessarily be able to see when passing them in a hallway.

Dreams

This project is a series of photographs that I took of the Brandeis University campus. I tried to show the campus completely desserted, something most people don't get the chance to see. These photos were taken during a foggy night around 4am - I attempted to make these places look as though they could only occur in a dream.
